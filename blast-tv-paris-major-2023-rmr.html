What is RMR?

The Regional Major Rankings, or RMR, function as the qualifying system for CS:GO Major Championships, beginning with ESL One: Rio 2020. They are divided into three regions: Asia-Pacific, Europe, and the Americas.

They emerged during the online era brought on by the COVID-19 pandemic, serving as an alternative to the CS:GO Minor Championships. These qualifiers do not have a monetary prize pool and only determine if participants get a spot at a Major event and the stage they will start from: Legends Stage or Challengers Stage.

BLAST.tv Paris Major Europe RMR Qualifiers Overview

A total of fifty-six teams will fight for spots at the BLAST.tv Paris Major 2023 from April 6th to April 15th, and only sixteen best line-ups will qualify for the event that will go down in history as the last CS:GO Major ever. Yep, you read it right, the next Major event will occur after Counter-Strike 2 launches, so this is the last one.

BLAST.tv Paris Major 2023 RMR events will be held in the Swiss System Format, with Elimination and Advancement matches played as Bo3 and all other matches as Bo1.

There will be four qualifying events for the BLAST.tv Paris Major 2023:

  • Europe RMR A
  • Europe RMR B

  • Asia RMR

Only eight teams will play in the Asia RMR, while other qualifiers will be attended by sixteen teams each. Let’s learn more about the participating line-ups and where to watch the upcoming matches.
