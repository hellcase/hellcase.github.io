How to Protect Yourself Against Online Frauds?

What is Credit Card Fraud?

Credit card fraud is a type of electronic fraud committed using a payment card, such as a credit card or debit card. The goal might be to pay into another account that is under criminal control or to receive goods or services using another person’s finances.

How Does Credit Card Fraud Happen?

Hellcase is a legit platform, but there are some other ways that you may face the risk of credit card fraud, and we feel the responsibility of mentioning a few important details about it. Credit card fraud takes place when an unauthorized individual accesses your information and uses it to make transactions.

Below, we have mentioned several types of credit card fraud, together with the ways to avoid them and the steps you have to follow if you already are a victim of credit card fraud.

Common Types of Credit Card Fraud ?

There are hundreds of different methods that can be used to commit an online crime. It is important to note that fraudsters are in constant competition with technological advancements implemented by financial intermediaries. The more technological barriers hackers and fraudsters have, the more creative frauds we will face.

Here are a few types of credit card fraud that internet users face frequently. Familiarizing yourself with each one of these will help you to be cautious, in case similar fraud is intended toward you.

Keep this in Mind!

  • Card-not-present (CNP) fraud – This kind of fraud is carried out through phone, postal, or internet transactions.
  • Counterfeit and skimming fraud – This sort of fraud is carried out by a criminal who copies or skims the information from a valid credit or debit card’s magnetic stripe and uses it to make a fake plastic card that has the information from the actual card.
  • Lost and stolen card fraud – This kind of fraud happens when a fraudster uses a lost or stolen card using a fake identity.
  • Card-never arrived-fraud – This kind of fraud happens when you request a new card, but thieves steal it from your mailbox or intercept it before you can get it.
  • False application fraud – This kind of fraud happens when someone applies for a loan, credit card, employment, or other service using stolen or fake information.

How to Prevent Credit Card Fraud ?

As mentioned above, there are hundreds of ways that fraudsters may want to trick us. Therefore, it is almost impossible to learn all the ways to prevent such an incident in the future. However, there are some basic precautions you can take to significantly reduce your chances of becoming a victim of a scam.

  • Safeguarding the credit card.
  • Keep an eye on online credit card transactions.
  • Prevent writing your credit card number somewhere in public.
  • Never sign unfilled receipts.
  • Turn on 2FA and reset all passwords periodically.
  • Verify all of your online transactions.
  • Report lost or stolen cards instantly reporting
